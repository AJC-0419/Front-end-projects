The State of the Roman Empire (Crisis of the Third Century)

Upon the assassination of emperor severus around 235 bc, the empire at that time was in a state of weakness over many factors:


  • The military getting more and more involved in the empire's politics
  • The succession of emperors being unclear and unstable throughtout the years (court intrigue plays a part)
  • The economy going through worse and worse inflation and depression
  • Numerous invasions of barbarians forcing emperors to increase the pressure on defend its borders. In turn also requiring larger armies, which reduced men available for the agricultural labor
  • The plague which brought further instability and fear to communities and cities

EMPEROR AURELIAN: Restorer of The World

    Reign (Quickly set about restoring Roman authority in Europe)

    • Repelled attacks in Pannonia, northern Italy, and in the danube regions (Vandals, Alemanni, and Jugunthi)
    • Ordered the construction of the "Auerlian Walls" to protect the capital against further barbarian attacks
    • In 271, he reclaimed eastern provinces from the palmyran empire which he did successfully after the capture of Septimia Zenobia
    • In 274, he beat the Gallic empire in the north that was carved out and was led by Tetricus, which after the battle of Chalons, was swiftly defeated and joined in the triumph march with zenobia.
    • In short, an Outstanding general and a severe and uncompromising administrator.
