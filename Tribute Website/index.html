The State of the Roman Empire (Crisis of the Third Century)

Following the assassination of the emperor Alexander Severus (222-235 CE) in 235 CE by his own troops, the crisis was started due to a number of factors that have grown in the empire throught the years:


  • Increased participation by the military in politics
  • Lack of adherence to a clear policy of succession for emperors
  • Inflation and economic depression
  • Increased pressure on the emperor to defend the provinces from invading tribes
  • The plague which heightened fears and destabilized communities
  • Larger armies which required more men and decreased the agricultural labor force.

EMPEROR AURELIAN: Restorer of The World

Reign (Quickly set about restoring Roman authority in Europe)

  • Born in 215 A.D, near the danube river
  • Repelled attacks from the Vandals in Pannonia
  • Expelled the Alemanni and Juthungi from northern Italy and chased the latter out of the Danube and later Quelled a revolt at the imperial mint
  • Ordered the construction of the "Auerlian Walls" to protect against barbarians
  • In 271, he sought to recover the eastern provinces from the palmyran empire which he did successfully after the capture of Septimia Zenobia (mother of the supposed emperor Wahballat)
  • In 274, he then focused on the Gallic empire in the north led by Tetricus, which after the battle of Chalons, was swiftly defeated and joined in the triumph march with zenobia.
  • In the same year, he then focused on the withdrawal of Roman troops from Dacia and resettle soldiers and settlers south of the Danube for the protection of the empire.
  • Outstanding general and a severe and uncompromising administrator.
